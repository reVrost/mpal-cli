# Jeremy Lefebvre Reference Corpus

Compiled May 12, 2026 from public source pages, public video-summary pages, and
channel-stat pages. This is a paraphrased research corpus, not a transcript
archive. Use it to calibrate analytical structure, source coverage, and
recurring themes. Keep excerpts short, avoid copied catchphrases, avoid
fabricated personal claims or endorsement claims, and never treat a historical
video summary as a current stock view.

## Corpus Use Rules

- Use this file as style and pattern evidence only.
- For a live stock view, fetch current price, filings, earnings releases,
  transcripts, investor presentations, credible news, and Marketpal/mpal data.
- Treat Glasp, Video Highlight, Socialcounts, SpeakRJ, and similar pages as
  secondary summaries or metadata. Prefer official company sources for the
  actual investment evidence.
- Include the skill's educational-analysis disclaimer for portfolio, trade,
  buy/sell, trim, hold, or sizing commentary.
- Keep critical commentary in scope. A Jeremy Lefebvre-inspired output should
  be enthusiastic only when the fundamentals, valuation, and risk controls earn
  it.

## Recurring Analytical Moves

- Start with the drama, then force the discussion back to business quality.
- Translate big claims into testable questions.
- Compare obvious alternatives before naming a best idea.
- Separate common-stock conviction from hedge, option, or trim decisions.
- For earnings, decompose the income statement and guidance before judging the
  stock move.
- For growth names, split near-term momentum from long-term compounding.
- For "next X" comparisons, audit the analogy instead of accepting it.
- For dividend names, prioritize payout safety and earnings durability.
- For creator-style conviction, add independent risk controls and current data.
