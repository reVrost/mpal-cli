# Portfolio Review Rubric

Use this rubric for formal portfolio reviews, portfolio cleanup, exit/trim policy work, and post-trade risk reviews. The goal is a professional portfolio-manager read: clear facts, disciplined risk, and actionable next steps.

## Portfolio-Level Score: 100 Points

| Category | Points | What Good Looks Like |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Objective realism | 10 | Return target translated into required sleeve returns, volatility, drawdown, and contribution assumptions. |
| Sleeve discipline | 15 | Core, engine, high-conviction, and cash exposures are mapped to policy targets and drift bands. |
| Concentration control | 15 | Top positions, top 5/top 10, and theme clusters are measured; oversized risks have explicit review triggers. |
| Thesis coherence | 15 | Holdings match the stated thesis without unmanaged duplication or off-thesis leftovers. |
| Risk/reward quality | 15 | Upside exposures are balanced against valuation, balance sheet, cash flow, liquidity, dilution, and event risk. |
| Exit/trim readiness | 15 | Portfolio has clear sell, trim, loss-control, and thesis-break rules before stress arrives. |
| Evidence quality | 10 | Uses current portfolio data, source-backed events, MarketPal signals, and labels stale or missing data. |
| Actionability | 5 | Ends with a small number of concrete next actions, not a vague list of observations. |

Score interpretation:

| Score | Rating | Interpretation |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 85-100 | Institutional quality | Coherent, disciplined, evidence-backed, and actionable. |
| 70-84 | Strong but watch risks | Good structure with one or two material weaknesses. |
| 55-69 | Speculative / fragile | Thesis may be valid, but concentration, exits, or evidence quality need work. |
| Below 55 | Uncontrolled risk | Portfolio needs cleanup before adding new exposure. |

## Holding-Level Score: 50 Points

| Category | Points | What Good Looks Like |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Thesis fit | 8 | Clear role in the user's stated portfolio thesis. |
| Exposure purity | 6 | Direct exposure to the intended driver, not a weak proxy. |
| Business quality | 8 | Strong margins, cash conversion, ROIC, balance sheet, or durable competitive position. |
| Valuation / payoff | 8 | Upside/downside is attractive versus growth, quality, and risk. |
| Catalyst and timing | 6 | There is a visible reason to own now, not just someday. |
| Position sizing | 8 | Size fits conviction, volatility, liquidity, correlation, and sleeve policy. |
| Exit clarity | 6 | Clear thesis-break, trim, and stop-review conditions exist. |

Action labels:

| Score | Label | Default Action |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 42-50 | Core engine hold/add candidate | Hold; add only if valuation, sizing, and validation support it. |
| 34-41 | Good hold | Hold; top up only if it remains above buy threshold and sizing permits. |
| 26-33 | Watch / prove-it | Hold small or watchlist; require catalyst before adding. |
| 18-25 | Trim candidate | Look for validated trims or replacement candidates. |
| Below 18 | Exit candidate | Exit unless there is a specific, current, evidence-backed reason to hold. |

## Risk Flags That Cap the Score

Apply these caps even if the story is attractive:

- No validated data or stale critical data: cap portfolio/holding actionability at "watch".
- Single active name above policy limit: cap portfolio score at 75 until reviewed.
- Theme cluster above working cap without written plan: cap portfolio score at 70.
- Financing, dilution, solvency, governance, or accounting concern: cap holding score at 25 unless resolved.
- No exit rule for a high-beta holding: cap holding score at 33.
- Averaging down after a drawdown without refreshed evidence and validation: cap process score at 60.
- Private holdings, transactions, or risk reports written to repo-tracked paths without explicit user request: cap portfolio score at 60.
- Durable policy mutation without explicit approval or dated backup: cap process score at 55.
- Missing freshness block for a live portfolio review: cap evidence quality at 6/10.

## Professional Review Standards

- Prefer fewer, higher-quality actions over many marginal ideas.
- Acknowledge when a high-return target requires high volatility and concentrated active risk.
- Treat cash as a position with optionality value, not a failure to be fully invested.
- Distinguish a high-conviction bet from an engine position. If a normal engine holding needs special treatment, explicitly reclassify it or resize it.
- Never confuse a good thesis with a good position size.
- Do not let recent price action alone justify larger sizing.
- Do not recommend adding to a crowded theme unless it replaces weaker exposure or reduces portfolio risk.
- Treat privacy and process control as part of portfolio risk management, not administrative cleanup.
