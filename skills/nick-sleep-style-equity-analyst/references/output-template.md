# Output Template

Use this template unless the user asks for a short answer, table, or memo.

Include this sentence when discussing portfolio actions, trades, buying,
selling, trimming, holding, or position sizing:

> This is educational analysis, not personal financial advice.

## 1. Quick Take

- One-sentence classification: Compounder / Watchlist / Too Hard / Avoid.
- One-sentence reason.
- Biggest missing evidence or risk.

## 2. Business Destination

- What the business could look like in 5-10 years.
- What has to be true for that destination to happen.

## 3. Customer Proposition

- What customers get today.
- Whether that proposition is improving with scale.

## 4. Scale Economies Shared

- Cost, service, selection, speed, trust, or convenience advantages.
- Evidence that scale benefits customers, not only shareholders.

## 5. Culture And Incentives

- Observable management behavior.
- Owner alignment, compensation, capital allocation, and communication quality.

## 6. Reinvestment Runway

- Where incremental capital can go.
- Expected return on that reinvestment and signs of saturation.

## 7. Financial Evidence

- Current revenue, growth, margins, cash flow, balance sheet, dilution, and
  disclosure quality.
- Separate reported accounting from look-through business progress.

## 8. Valuation And Prospective Return

- Current price and valuation.
- Conservative, base, and upside scenario assumptions.
- Whether the price leaves enough prospective return.

## 9. What The Market May Be Missing

- Short-term noise that may obscure long-term progress.
- Underappreciated customer, culture, or reinvestment evidence.

## 10. Risks To The Destination

- The clearest derailers.
- Evidence that would change the view.

## 11. Bull Case

- What has to go right.
- Why the payoff could be large.

## 12. Bear Case

- What could go wrong.
- Why the current price may already discount too much.

## 13. What I'd Watch For Five Years

- Three to six durable evidence points.
- Avoid over-weighting one-quarter noise unless it changes the thesis.
