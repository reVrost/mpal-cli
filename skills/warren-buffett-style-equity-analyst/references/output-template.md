# Output Template

Use this structure for formal analysis unless the user asks for a shorter response.

## Quick Take

- Educational opinion: Attractive / Watchlist / Too Hard / Avoid.
- One-sentence reason.
- Include: "This is educational analysis, not personal financial advice" when discussing buy/sell/trim/hold/sizing.

## Business Understandability

- How the company makes money.
- What must be forecast.
- Circle-of-competence judgment.

## Moat And Competitive Position

- Moat type.
- Evidence that it is widening, stable, or shrinking.
- Main competitive threat.

## Management And Capital Allocation

- Incentives and candor.
- Reinvestment, acquisitions, buybacks, dividends, debt, and share issuance.
- Per-share value creation record.

## Economics: Margins, ROIC, Cash Flow

- Revenue and margin history.
- ROIC/ROE and leverage adjustment.
- Free cash flow and owner-earnings quality.
- Capital intensity and reinvestment runway.

## Balance Sheet And Downside Risk

- Debt, maturities, liquidity, covenants, dilution risk.
- Stress case.
- Biggest permanent-capital-loss risk.

## Intrinsic Value Range

- Key assumptions.
- Downside/base/upside value range.
- Explain uncertainty and avoid false precision.

## Margin Of Safety

- Current price versus conservative value.
- Whether the discount compensates for uncertainty.

## Opportunity Cost

- Compare with cash, index exposure, and obvious alternative holdings.

## Bull Case

- What must go right.

## Bear Case

- What breaks the thesis.

## What Would Change The View

- Evidence that would move the opinion up or down.

## Missing Data

- Primary sources or metrics still needed.

## Educational Opinion

- Attractive / Watchlist / Too Hard / Avoid.
- Keep the final answer direct and consistent with the evidence.
