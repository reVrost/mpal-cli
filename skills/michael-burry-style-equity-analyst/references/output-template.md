# Output Template

Use this template unless the user asks for a shorter answer, table, memo, or
portfolio-level review.

## Required Disclaimer

Include this sentence for portfolio, trade, short, hedge, option, buy, sell,
trim, hold, or position-sizing commentary:

This is educational analysis, not personal financial advice.

## Default Single-Security Format

### 1. Quick Take

- Verdict: Long / Short Candidate / Hedge / Watchlist / Avoid / Too Hard.
- One-sentence reason.
- Evidence confidence: High / Medium / Low.

### 2. Consensus View

- What the market appears to believe.
- Main bullish and bearish narrative.
- Why the consensus might be lazy, crowded, or roughly right.

### 3. Variant Perception

- The non-consensus claim.
- The primary-source evidence that supports it.
- What the market would need to recognize.

### 4. Filing Evidence

- Latest filings and source dates checked.
- Most important filing facts.
- Gaps or stale evidence.

### 5. Accounting and Cash Flow Quality

- GAAP versus adjusted results.
- Cash conversion, working capital, capitalized costs, reserves, impairments,
  stock compensation, and dilution.
- Any accounting clues that contradict the headline story.

### 6. Balance Sheet and Liquidity

- Cash, debt, leases, maturities, covenants, liquidity, and refinancing risk.
- Stress case.
- Management's ability to dilute, refinance, sell assets, cut spending, or
  change capital returns.

### 7. Valuation / Asset Value / Downside Math

- Market cap and enterprise value.
- Conservative asset value, normalized earnings power, or downside scenario.
- Upside/downside range and key assumptions.

### 8. Catalyst Path

- What forces recognition.
- Expected timing.
- Pre-catalyst evidence to monitor.

### 9. Position Structure and Path Risk

- Common equity, put, spread, pair, basket hedge, credit instrument, or no
  position.
- Borrow/options/liquidity/squeeze/carry risks.
- Why the structure matches the thesis.

### 10. Bull Case

- Best evidence-backed case against the skeptical view.

### 11. Bear Case

- Best evidence-backed downside case.

### 12. What Would Falsify The Thesis

- Specific facts, numbers, filing items, or events that would change the view.

### 13. Educational Opinion

- Final label: Long / Short Candidate / Hedge / Watchlist / Avoid / Too Hard.
- Clear, bounded conclusion.

## Portfolio Or 13F Read Format

1. Scope and data limitations.
2. Current source dates and filings checked.
3. Holdings or exposures visible in public data.
4. What can be inferred cautiously.
5. What cannot be inferred.
6. Crowding, liquidity, options, and concentration risks.
7. Educational takeaways for the user's portfolio or watchlist.

## Bubble / Market-Risk Read Format

1. Asset or theme under review.
2. Consensus narrative.
3. Evidence of excess, crowding, leverage, or accounting strain.
4. Primary-source counterevidence.
5. Transmission mechanism: how the bubble could deflate.
6. Timing and path risk.
7. Hedge candidates or "avoid only" conclusion.
8. Falsification evidence.
