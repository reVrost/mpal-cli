# Scion Theme Index

This index maps recurring public Scion/Burry analytical themes to practical
questions for current public-equity work. Use it as a navigation aid, then
verify every live claim with current sources.

## Filing-First Research

Public anchor:

- NYT op-ed and Vanderbilt transcript both emphasize research into market
  mechanics and documents before the crisis view hardened.
- SEC filings provide observable Scion positions, activist letters, and manager
  metadata.

Use in analysis:

- Which filing or contract best proves or disproves the market story?
- Which footnote, schedule, exhibit, or proxy table matters more than the press
  release?
- Are adjusted metrics reconcilable to cash and GAAP results?

## Asset Value Versus Market Price

Public anchor:

- Public Scion/issuer communications around GameStop and Tailored Brands focused
  on capital allocation, share repurchases, debt reduction, and per-share value.

Use in analysis:

- What are tangible assets, net cash, working capital, real estate, brands,
  subsidiaries, tax assets, or liquidation value worth under conservative
  assumptions?
- Does a buyback, asset sale, debt paydown, or dividend change unlock value or
  create fragility?
- Does the market price imply a liquidation, recovery, or decline scenario that
  is inconsistent with primary evidence?

## Catalyst Discipline

Public anchor:

- GameStop/Tailored Brands letters show explicit asks rather than vague hope.
- Subprime thesis had a timing/mechanism component tied to mortgage product
  quality, resets, credit derivatives, and market structure.

Use in analysis:

- What forces recognition: maturity wall, covenant test, earnings reset,
  regulatory decision, capital return, asset sale, management change, or market
  liquidity event?
- Can the catalyst occur before carry, borrow cost, or business decay ruins the
  setup?
- What date range matters?

## Accounting Skepticism

Public anchor:

- Burry's public crisis explanation centers on looking through securitization
  structures and mortgage quality instead of relying on ratings or official
  optimism.

Use in analysis:

- Do earnings convert to free cash flow?
- Are reserves, impairments, capitalization policies, receivables, inventory,
  leases, stock compensation, or restructuring charges distorting reality?
- Does management lean on adjusted metrics that exclude recurring costs?

## Balance Sheet And Liquidity Stress

Public anchor:

- Public Scion letters and 13D activity repeatedly connect capital allocation to
  debt, liquidity, repurchases, and shareholder value.

Use in analysis:

- What cash is truly available after operating needs, leases, capex, restricted
  cash, and debt service?
- What maturities, covenants, variable-rate debt, supplier financing, or margin
  obligations can force bad outcomes?
- Can management dilute, refinance, or sell assets in a way that changes the
  thesis?

## Bubble Diagnosis And Crowding

Public anchor:

- Public Burry commentary has repeatedly focused on bubbles, passive flows,
  credit excess, and market structures where price signals can fail.

Use in analysis:

- Is the buyer base extrapolating a narrative rather than underwriting cash
  flows?
- Are passive/index flows, retail options activity, short covering, private
  financing, or sell-side incentives distorting price discovery?
- Is the contrarian trade itself now crowded?

## Short / Put Asymmetry

Public anchor:

- Scion's public 13F history includes reported put/call line items in some
  periods. The subprime case involved asymmetric downside instruments, not just
  vague bearishness.

Use in analysis:

- Why is the instrument better than simply avoiding the stock?
- What are max loss, carry, implied volatility, borrow, liquidity, and expiry
  risks?
- Is there enough catalyst density before the option or hedge decays?

## Management Incentives And Insider Behavior

Public anchor:

- Activist-style letters to boards focused on capital allocation and governance.

Use in analysis:

- Are executives paid for per-share value, revenue growth, adjusted EBITDA,
  stock price, or empire building?
- Are insiders buying, selling, pledging, exercising, or receiving large grants?
- Does board behavior align with outside shareholders?

## Position Sizing Under Volatility

Public anchor:

- Public accounts of Scion's crisis trade show that being early can create major
  interim stress. Public filings show concentrated, changing positions, but not
  full risk exposures.

Use in analysis:

- How much can the position move against the thesis before the catalyst?
- What is the liquidity under stress?
- Does the idea deserve a watchlist slot, small asymmetric option, pair trade, or
  no position?

## Too Hard

Public anchor:

- The public style prizes independent evidence, not constant activity.

Use in analysis:

- If accounting is opaque, incentives are unclear, or the catalyst depends on
  unknowable macro timing, say "too hard."
- State the exact missing data that would make the setup analyzable.
