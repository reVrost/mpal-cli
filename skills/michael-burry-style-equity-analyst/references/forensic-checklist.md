# Forensic Checklist

Use this checklist when the task involves accounting quality, distress, shorts,
hedges, controversial longs, bubbles, or event-driven mispricing.

## Evidence Triage

- Identify the exact security: common, preferred, bond, option, ADR, parent,
  subsidiary, SPAC, holding company, or basket.
- Pull the latest price, market cap, enterprise value, short interest, borrow
  context, option chain context, and volume/liquidity where relevant.
- Read the latest 10-K/20-F, 10-Q, 8-Ks, proxy, earnings release, transcript,
  investor deck, and debt documents.
- Identify stale facts and source dates.
- Separate source-backed facts from estimates and judgment calls.

## Accounting Quality

- Compare net income, operating cash flow, free cash flow, and adjusted EBITDA.
- Check revenue recognition, deferred revenue, billings, receivables growth,
  days sales outstanding, contract assets, and customer concentration.
- Review gross margin, warranty reserves, returns, inventory turns, write-downs,
  and channel stuffing risk.
- Review capitalized software, capitalized interest, capitalized customer costs,
  depreciation lives, impairment history, and restructuring add-backs.
- Check stock-based compensation, dilution, share count creep, buybacks versus
  issuance, and option exercises.
- Read segment notes for cross-subsidies, hidden losses, and non-core assets.
- Compare management's adjusted metrics with recurring economic costs.

## Balance Sheet And Liquidity

- Calculate cash, restricted cash, revolver availability, debt, leases, pension
  obligations, purchase commitments, working-capital needs, and near-term
  maturities.
- Read covenant definitions, leverage tests, interest coverage, springing
  covenants, borrowing-base limits, and change-of-control provisions.
- Stress EBITDA, gross margin, capex, working capital, and refinancing rates.
- Ask whether management can dilute, sell assets, suspend dividends, cut capex,
  refinance, or amend covenants.
- Check supplier financing, factoring, customer prepayments, and off-balance
  sheet obligations.

## Asset Value And Downside Math

- Build conservative asset value: cash, working capital, inventory haircuts,
  real estate, securities, brands, tax assets, subsidiaries, and liquidation
  value.
- Compare asset value with debt seniority and claims.
- For operating businesses, normalize earnings across a cycle and penalize
  structurally declining revenue.
- Avoid using peak margins or boom multiples as base-case proof.
- State downside before upside.

## Short / Hedge Structure

- Define why the structure is better than avoiding the stock.
- Check borrow availability, borrow cost, recall risk, float, days to cover,
  ownership concentration, and retail/options participation.
- Review option liquidity, implied volatility, skew, spreads, expiry, and
  catalyst calendar.
- Limit unlimited-loss structures unless the setup has very clear risk controls.
- Test whether a squeeze, takeover, index inclusion, capital raise, buyback, or
  unexpected refinance can break the trade.

## Catalyst Path

- Identify the event that forces recognition: earnings, guidance, debt maturity,
  covenant test, regulatory decision, litigation date, product cycle, asset sale,
  buyback, dividend change, index event, lockup, or financing.
- Estimate timing and whether it arrives before carry/decay becomes punitive.
- State what evidence would confirm progress before the catalyst.
- State what would falsify the catalyst path.

## Crowding And Sentiment

- Check ownership, sell-side ratings, short interest, options volume, social
  attention, ETF/passive ownership, and insider activity.
- Ask whether the consensus is truly lazy or whether the market already knows
  the problem.
- Avoid "contrarian" labels when the trade is already popular.

## Final Gate

- Can the thesis be explained with primary-source evidence in five sentences?
- Is there a measurable valuation or downside framework?
- Is there a catalyst, or is this just "cheap" or "expensive"?
- Are path risks explicit?
- Is the final label one of: Long, Short Candidate, Hedge, Watchlist, Avoid, or
  Too Hard?
