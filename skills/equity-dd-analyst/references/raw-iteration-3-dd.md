- **Confidence:** **Medium-high** for exposure ranking; **medium** for valuation because market prices are current Marketpal data and contractor earnings visibility depends on project execution.

**Company Notes**

**SXE.AX - Southern Cross Electrical Engineering**

SXE is a diversified engineering contractor, not an owner/operator. Its edge is multi-disciplinary delivery: electrical, communications, security, fire, switchboards and maintenance. The filed December 2025 release showed DigiCo SYD1 awards covering electrical, fire services and switchboards for the 20MW deployment project. The H1 FY26 filed commentary says data-centre revenue exceeded $120m in FY25, a similar volume is expected in FY26, and the group is tendering more than $1bn of future DC work.

Financially, SXE has the most balanced profile in this screen. Filed H1 FY26 results show revenue of $349.1m, underlying EBITDA of $35.4m, order book of $710m and FY26 underlying EBITDA guidance of at least $72m, while the statutory loss was driven by the WestConnex arbitration cost. Marketpal-derived facts: price $4.09, market cap $1.09bn, forward P/E 24.5, QVM score 82.1, profile updated 9 May 2026. On an approximate net-cash EV of $1.03bn, FY26 EV/EBITDA is about 14x. That is not cheap for a contractor, but the balance sheet and DC pipeline justify a premium if margins hold.

Bull case: DC tender conversion, cross-selling between Heyday/Trivantage/MDE/Force Fire, and FY27 revenue step-up from the current tender book. Bear case: project execution, warranty/claims risk, and momentum investors exiting if DC awards slow.

**SKS.AX - SKS Technologies**

SKS is the highest-beta contractor exposure. The April 2026 filed release states SKS expanded the MEL02A hyperscale data-centre contract to $210m, covering 126MW, with completion expected in 3Q27. Work on hand was $350m, with $240m flowing into FY27, and FY26 forecasts were revenue of $340m and 10% PBT margin.

The filed 1H26 release shows sales revenue of $131.75m, EBITDA of $14.02m, PBT of $12.80m, NPAT of $8.81m, cash from operations of $27.84m and cash reserves just under $52m. It also states repeat business of about 90% and a debt-free position. Marketpal-derived facts: price $8.04, market cap $927m, forward P/E 27.0, QVM score 76.1, profile updated 9 May 2026. My valuation read is harsher than Marketpal’s forward P/E: using company PBT guidance, SKS is around 27x pre-tax earnings, or roughly high-30s P/E on a normal tax basis.

Bull case: SKS is becoming a preferred electrical contractor for hyperscale facilities, and NSW expansion may broaden the runway. Bear case: the order book is now dominated by larger, longer-duration data-centre work; any delivery issue or margin miss would matter disproportionately.

**IFT.AX - Infratil**

IFT is the best asset-quality exposure because CDC is a scaled owner/operator, but it is not a pure listed data-centre REIT; it is a holding company with other infrastructure assets. Infratil says CDC signed a 555MW, 30-year contract with a US high-end investment-grade customer, taking total contracted capacity above 1GW. CDC expects FY28 EBITDAF above A$1bn, and about A$2bn annualised EBITDAF when fully deployed. Infratil owns 49.7% of CDC.

The critical funding point is that CDC expects FY27 capex of A$3.8-4.2bn excluding land, funded by cash, committed debt facilities and further debt/hybrid issuance; the company says the new contract does not require further shareholder equity. CDC’s March 2026 independent valuation was A$15.0bn midpoint, and Infratil’s 49.72% interest was valued at A$7.454bn. Marketpal-derived facts: price A$12.40, market cap A$12.37bn, QVM score 33.1, profile updated 9 May 2026. That means the CDC stake alone is about 60% of Infratil’s listed market cap before considering One NZ, Longroad, Contact, Kao and other holdings, but also before holding-company debt, fees and portfolio discount.

Bull case: CDC keeps converting hyperscale demand into long-term contracted capacity, and credit-market access lowers equity funding risk. Bear case: capex inflation, power/grid constraints, higher debt cost, and valuation duration risk.

**VNT.AX - Ventia**

VNT is a quality infrastructure-services company, but the weakest data-centre theme. FY25 filings show revenue of $6.141bn, EBITDA of $532.1m, NPATA of $257.6m, work in hand of $22.1bn, 87% of 2026 revenue secured and 93.6% cash conversion. Net debt/EBITDA was 1.3x, liquidity was $636m, and the company had S&P BBB and Moody’s Baa2 ratings.

Data centres sit under the broader “digital infrastructure” opportunity: Ventia cites data centres, high-density fibre and ICT as areas where it has lifecycle capabilities. However, it does not disclose DC revenue or backlog. The May 2026 $340m Victorian road-maintenance award and the $250m buyback are useful catalysts, but not data-centre catalysts. Marketpal-derived facts: price $5.72, market cap $4.70bn, forward P/E 15.6, FCF yield 5.7%, QVM score 78.1. Note: Marketpal’s VNT event stream contained some Vontier SEC-related items; I used company-filed Ventia sources for operating facts.

Bull case: steady government-backed work, buyback support and margin discipline. Bear case: limited data-centre earnings sensitivity and retendering/government budget risk.

**Cross-Company Read**

The key distinction is **asset ownership versus project services**. IFT owns the scarce asset through CDC, so it has the best strategic exposure and longest contract duration. SXE and SKS sell into the build-out, so they can grow faster on less capital but carry project and working-capital risk. VNT has the best defensive services profile, but weakest DC purity.

On valuation-adjusted risk/reward, SXE screens better than SKS because it has a broader order book, net cash, lower apparent earnings multiple and less reliance on one large hyperscale program. SKS has the cleaner “small-cap DC contractor” narrative, but that narrative is already capitalised aggressively. IFT is the cleanest way to underwrite the long-term value of Australasian data centres, but the next 12-24 months are about funding, delivery and whether the market accepts CDC’s independent valuation assumptions.
