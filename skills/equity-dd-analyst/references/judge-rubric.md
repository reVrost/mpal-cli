# Equity DD Judge Rubric

Score each category 1-5. A strong final answer should average at least 4.0 and must score at least 4 on source discipline, comparison consistency, and conclusion usefulness.

## 1. Source Discipline

- **5:** Current primary sources used for key facts; dates and links included; secondary sources clearly labeled with primary verification target when needed.
- **3:** Some current sources used, but primary evidence or dates are incomplete.
- **1:** Relies mostly on memory, unsupported claims, or stale information.

## 2. Exposure Classification

- **5:** Correctly distinguishes direct, indirect, contractor, investor, and non-core exposure.
- **3:** Mentions exposure but does not quantify or classify consistently.
- **1:** Treats all names as equivalent thematic plays.

## 3. Financial Analysis

- **5:** Covers growth, margins, cash flow, leverage, capex/funding, valuation, and risk, including look-through math for holding companies and backlog/cash-conversion mechanics for contractors where relevant.
- **3:** Covers some financial metrics but misses funding or valuation implications.
- **1:** Narrative-only analysis with little financial substance.

## 4. Comparison Consistency

- **5:** Every company is compared on the same framework, scored consistently, the weighting method for the overall verdict is clear, and evidence confidence is visible by ticker.
- **3:** Uses a matrix but applies uneven evidence.
- **1:** Standalone company notes without a real comparison.

## 5. Investment Debate

- **5:** Bull case, bear case, catalysts, and disconfirming evidence are clear.
- **3:** Has risks and positives but weak debate.
- **1:** One-sided conclusion.

## 6. Conclusion Usefulness

- **5:** Simple conclusion is specific, ranked, and tied to evidence.
- **3:** Conclusion exists but is generic or hedged.
- **1:** No usable answer to what matters most.

## 7. MarketPal Integration

- **5:** Uses `mpal` where available, timestamps or labels MarketPal-derived facts, and clearly notes gaps.
- **3:** Uses some `mpal` data but misses obvious available commands.
- **1:** Ignores available MarketPal data.
