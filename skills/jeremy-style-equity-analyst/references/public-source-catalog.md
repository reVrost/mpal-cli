# Public Source Catalog

This catalog stores public reference links and paraphrased research notes for
the `jeremy-style-equity-analyst` skill. It is intentionally not a transcript
archive. Use it to remember source coverage, recurring analytical patterns, and
style calibration.

## Use Rules

- Do not copy full transcripts into repo memory.
- Do not imitate Jeremy's identity, exact catchphrases, or private views.
- Treat third-party summaries as style and research-direction evidence only.
- For current stock analysis, fetch fresh market data, filings, earnings
  releases, transcripts, investor presentations, and credible news before
  making a view.
- For any portfolio, trade, buy/sell, trim, hold, or sizing commentary, include
  the educational-analysis disclaimer required by the skill.

## Transcript And Summary Pages

### I Just Bought 2 Growth Stocks And 3 Dividend Stocks

URL: https://glasp.co/youtube/LEMsrQChTHo

Published: November 14, 2025.

Notes:

- Current-market drama is used as the opening frame, with Bitcoin and many
  stocks down sharply in a short period.
- Buying activity is discussed through a mix of growth and dividend stocks.
- Useful pattern: treat volatility as a chance to reassess opportunity, but
  require the analyst to separate price drama from business quality.

### AMD, CRM, ADBE Emergency

URL: https://glasp.co/youtube/PUZZ8jZ156M

Published: January 13, 2026.

Notes:

- Multi-stock reaction format covering several major software or semiconductor
  names in one video.
- Useful pattern: start with why the market is reacting, then give a separate
  quick read for each company rather than forcing one broad conclusion.
- Good reference for earnings/news-response prompts.

### Investing $3.6Mil Into This 1 Stock

URL: https://glasp.co/youtube/gnjMDH3S0Ms

Published: July 2, 2025.

Notes:

- Public-account and large-position framing are used to make the analysis feel
  concrete.
- Summary describes a five-year single-stock thought process comparing Meta,
  Google, AMD, and other opportunities.
- Useful pattern: when asked for a best idea, compare obvious alternatives on
  durability, balance sheet, upside, and downside, then explain why one offers
  the best risk-adjusted setup.

### Your MUST Own This Stock

URL: https://glasp.co/youtube/_7CgnGD0124

Published: October 21, 2025.

Notes:

- High-conviction framing around a single stock and public-account movement.
- Useful pattern: if using strong language, support it with business quality,
  valuation, balance sheet, and catalyst evidence, not excitement alone.

### AMD And Lululemon

URL: https://glasp.co/youtube/66SB2dmJ98M

Published: September 5, 2025.

Notes:

- Reaction to AMD stock being down sharply, then a wider discussion of what is
  happening and whether it matters.
- Useful pattern: after a large move, ask whether the cause is fundamentals,
  guidance, expectations, valuation, or investor emotion.

### Jeremy Sells All His Stock

URL: https://glasp.co/youtube/FwEZX11mMf4

Published: February 28, 2022.

Notes:

- Summary focuses on short-seller behavior, negative narratives, and a specific
  company under pressure.
- Mentions revenue growth, product expansion, international opportunity,
  innovation, margin expansion, and path to profitability as thesis anchors.
- Useful pattern: bearish pressure should be checked against business evidence,
  but the skill must not dismiss bearish claims without verification.

### Confronting Jeremy Financial Education

URL: https://glasp.co/youtube/cwd5um2ZLx8

Published: October 11, 2020.

Notes:

- Interview summary emphasizes long-term growth potential, strong business
  fundamentals, balance sheet quality, and knowing the products/services.
- Reported red flags include bad balance sheet, mismanagement, and insufficient
  cash or investments.
- Useful pattern: include consumer/product understanding as a supporting lens,
  but do not let familiarity substitute for filings and numbers.

### From The Bottom Of My Heart, Thank You

URL: https://glasp.co/youtube/p/from-the-bottom-of-my-heart-thank-you-jeremy

Published: January 19, 2024.

Notes:

- Summary emphasizes market sentiment shifts, investor confidence during tough
  periods, and staying focused despite public opinion.
- Mentions CEO communication, earnings, guidance, and advertising performance
  as relevant stock-performance drivers.
- Useful pattern: sentiment can change quickly, so explain whether a stock's
  narrative has improved because facts improved or because the market changed
  its mood.
