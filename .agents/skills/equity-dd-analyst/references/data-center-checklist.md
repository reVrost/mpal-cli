# Data-Center Equity DD Checklist

Use this reference for data-center, digital infrastructure, telecom infrastructure, and data-center-adjacent stock comparisons.

## Exposure Classification

- **Pure-play owner/operator:** owns or operates data-center facilities and sells capacity or colocation directly.
- **Infrastructure investor:** owns stakes in data-center platforms or digital infrastructure through listed or unlisted holdings.
- **Contractor/services provider:** earns project, maintenance, engineering, power, cooling, or facilities revenue from data-center customers.
- **Network/telco adjacent:** benefits from connectivity, fiber, mobile, cloud interconnect, or enterprise IT demand.
- **Indirect/non-core:** data centers are a narrative or small customer segment, not the main economic exposure.

## Core DD Questions

- What percentage of earnings, assets, backlog, or growth capex is actually data-center related?
- Is revenue recurring, contracted, pass-through, project-based, or speculative development income?
- Who are the customers, how concentrated are they, and are they hyperscale/cloud/AI customers or enterprise colocation customers?
- What is the contract duration, escalation structure, utilization, renewal profile, and churn risk?
- Does the company control land, power, cooling, permits, fiber, and expansion capacity?
- What are the committed and uncommitted capex requirements?
- Are returns on invested capital visible after power, construction, financing, and tenant incentive costs?
- Can the balance sheet fund the pipeline without excessive dilution or leverage?
- What is the development, construction, grid-connection, and customer-delivery risk?
- What is the competitive position versus hyperscale self-build, global data-center REITs, telcos, and local specialists?

## Red Flags

- Data-center story is large in narrative but small in earnings.
- Growth depends on unfunded capex or repeated equity raises.
- Reported EBITDA rises while free cash flow stays structurally negative.
- High customer concentration with weak disclosure.
- Contracted capacity is confused with powered shell, land bank, or aspirational pipeline.
- Valuation assumes stabilized data-center economics before assets are stabilized.

## Positive Signals

- High contracted utilization with credible customers.
- Power and land secured before demand is marketed.
- Clear funding plan and leverage ceiling.
- Evidence that incremental projects earn returns above cost of capital.
- Management discloses capacity, utilization, capex, yields, and contract structure consistently.
