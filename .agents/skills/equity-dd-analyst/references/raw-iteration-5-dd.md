**Company Notes**

**SXE.AX - Southern Cross Electrical Engineering**

**Business and exposure:** SXE is a specialised electrical, instrumentation, communications, security, fire, maintenance and manufacturing contractor. Company-filed H1 FY26 materials state data centres are a structural tailwind: SXE businesses worked at eight data centres owned by six hyperscale cloud providers during the period, turned over A$120m of data-centre work in FY25, forecast similar in FY26, and were tendering on >A$1bn of new DC work over construction periods. Source: [SXE H1 FY26 presentation](https://data-api.marketindex.com.au/api/v1/announcements/XASX%3ASXE%3A6A1312509/pdf/inline/investor-presentation-h1-fy26-results).

**Financial position:** Company-filed facts show A$349.1m H1 FY26 revenue, underlying EBITDA A$35.4m, A$58.8m cash, nil debt, and A$150m combined facilities capacity with A$53.2m used. Order book was A$710m, up 6% pcp. Underlying FY26 EBITDA guidance was raised to at least A$72m.

**MarketPal-derived facts:** MarketPal snapshot dated 9 May 2026 shows price A$4.09, market cap A$1.09bn, EV A$1.04bn, forward P/E 24.5x, 52-week position near the high, and QVM score 82.1 with momentum 100. MarketPal FY2025 financials show revenue A$801.5m, EBITDA A$57.4m, FCF A$59.8m, cash A$88.6m, and total debt A$8.2m.

**Valuation:** On company FY26 EBITDA guidance of at least A$72m and MarketPal EV of ~A$1.04bn, SXE trades around **14.4x FY26 guided EBITDA** before any upside from FY27 DC tender conversion. That is not cheap for a contractor, but it is more defensible than SKS because SXE has broader discipline coverage, net cash, bonding capacity and diversified infrastructure exposure.

**Valuation stress:** If FY26 EBITDA is A$72m and the market applies a 10x contractor multiple, implied EV is A$720m, roughly 30% below MarketPal EV. To justify today’s EV at 10x, SXE needs EBITDA to move toward ~A$104m, which likely requires real FY27 DC and infrastructure conversion.

**Bull case:** Data-centre tendering converts; multi-discipline model wins larger packages; net cash and bonding capacity let SXE scale without equity dilution.

**Bear case:** DC construction margins compress; order book quality deteriorates; WestConnex-like project/legal issues reappear; valuation multiple normalises.

**Evidence confidence:** **High** - primary company presentation gives DC revenue, pipeline, balance sheet and guidance; MarketPal valuation is current.

**SKS.AX - SKS Technologies Group**

**Business and exposure:** SKS is an electrical technologies and digital infrastructure contractor. Company-filed April 2026 release states SKS secured an additional A$80m of work, lifting an existing data-centre contract to A$210m with Hickory for the MEL02A hyperscale facility in Melbourne’s western region. The project expanded from 90MW to 126MW and completion is expected in 3Q27. Source: [SKS ASX release, 17 April 2026](https://announcements.asx.com.au/asxpdf/20260417/pdf/06yl2p0dc3cj60.pdf).

**Financial position:** Company-filed April release states current work on hand is A$350m, with ~A$240m or 68.6% flowing into FY27, and supports FY26 forecasts of A$340m revenue and 10% PBT margin, or A$34m PBT. MarketPal event data also reports a May 2026 CBA bank guarantee facility increase to total facilities of A$52m supporting a A$355m order book and A$1.25bn pipeline; this should be rechecked against the ASX facility announcement.

**MarketPal-derived facts:** MarketPal snapshot dated 9 May 2026 shows price A$8.04, market cap A$927m, EV A$878m, trailing P/E 53.6x, forward P/E 27.0x, 1-year return 416%, 3-month return 106.9%, and QVM classification “Expensive Quality.” MarketPal FY2025 financials show revenue A$261.7m, EBITDA A$23.7m, net income A$13.8m, OCF A$35.0m, FCF A$32.4m, cash A$32.5m and total debt A$8.4m.

**Valuation:** Against company FY26 PBT guidance of A$34m, SKS’s MarketPal market cap of A$927m implies roughly **27x pre-tax earnings** before tax and before execution risk. That is a demanding multiple for a contractor, even with genuine hyperscale exposure.

**Contractor mechanics:** The A$210m MEL02A contract alone is ~60% of the A$350m work-on-hand figure. That is powerful visibility, but it also creates project concentration. Bank guarantee/bonding capacity matters more than accounting profit; the reported A$52m guarantee facility is therefore strategically important.

**Valuation stress:** If FY26 PBT is A$34m and tax normalises at 30%, NPAT would be ~A$24m. A 20x earnings multiple would imply ~A$476m equity value, around half the MarketPal market cap. To sustain today’s valuation at 25x after-tax earnings, SKS needs a credible path to ~A$37m NPAT, likely requiring continued FY27 order-book growth and stable contract margins.

**Bull case:** SKS becomes the preferred mid-cap electrical contractor for hyperscale DC work; A$1.25bn pipeline converts; facility increase avoids equity dilution.

**Bear case:** One large project delay or margin miss overwhelms the story; market de-rates contractor multiples; working capital/bonding limits growth.

**Evidence confidence:** **Medium-high** - primary contract and guidance evidence is strong; facility/pipeline detail is MarketPal event-derived and should be verified against the ASX facility release.

**IFT.AX - Infratil**

**Business and exposure:** Infratil is an infrastructure holding company, not a pure listed data-centre operator. The data-centre exposure is look-through CDC. Company-filed CDC materials state Infratil owns 49.72% of CDC and contributed A$250m of a A$500m shareholder funding round in February 2026. Source: [CDC contract update presentation](https://infratil.com/news/cdc-signs-555mw-data-centre-contract-with-us-customer/cdc-contract-update-growing-beyond-1gw/).

**Data-centre quality:** CDC is the best underlying asset in this comparison. Company-filed materials say the new customer is high-end investment grade, total contracted capacity grows to >1GW, 572MW is under construction, CDC has 2.9GW of built-MW capacity pipeline, and the platform expects FY28 EBITDAF above A$1bn. When fully deployed, 1GW contracted capacity would produce annualised contracted EBITDAF of ~A$2bn.

**Financial position:** Company-filed facts show CDC had A$3.9bn cash and undrawn bank borrowings at 31 March 2026, FY26 weighted average cost of debt around 6%, a Baa2 stable rating for CDC Australia, and FY27 capex guidance lifted to A$3.8-4.2bn excluding land. Infratil FY2025 annual report also shows digital infrastructure was 66% of portfolio value, CDC was the largest digital asset, and Infratil invested A$494m into CDC during FY2025. Source: [Infratil FY2025 annual report](https://www.marketscreener.com/quote/stock/INFRATIL-LIMITED-6494631/news/Infratil-FY2025-Annual-Report-50079351/).

**MarketPal-derived facts:** MarketPal snapshot dated 9 May 2026 shows IFT.AX price A$12.40, market cap A$12.37bn, EV A$20.09bn, P/E 62x, EV/EBIT 28.6x, EV/FCF negative, FCF yield near zero, and QVM classification “Avoid.” MarketPal FY2025 financials show revenue A$3.35bn, EBITDA A$862.8m, net loss A$286.3m and FCF of -A$211.9m, reflecting portfolio growth capex and holding-company accounting noise.

**Valuation:** IFT is a quality-through-NAV story rather than a clean earnings multiple. The market is paying for CDC’s growth visibility. The problem is that the new 555MW deal also increases capex intensity and execution exposure before full billing. CDC’s A$1bn+ FY28 EBITDAF guide is powerful, but Infratil shareholders own it through a leveraged, diversified holdco.

**Look-through stress:** If CDC reaches A$2bn annualised EBITDAF when 1GW is fully deployed, Infratil’s 49.72% share is ~A$994m EBITDAF before holdco costs, tax, minority issues and valuation discount. At 15x, that look-through EBITDAF would imply ~A$14.9bn gross CDC value to Infratil; at 12x it is ~A$11.9bn. The multiple matters enormously, and build timing plus funding cost can move NAV by billions.

**Bull case:** CDC’s 30-year contracted demand lowers utilisation risk; Baa2 rating opens cheaper debt markets; no further shareholder equity needed for the new contract under current plan.

**Bear case:** Grid/power/build delays push activation right; FY27 capex swells; debt costs stay high; the holdco trades at or below NAV after the post-announcement rerating.

**Evidence confidence:** **High** - primary CDC/Infratil materials are detailed and current; MarketPal valuation is current but holdco accounting requires caution.

**Valuation:** Ventia is the cheapest and most resilient name on conventional metrics: ~15.6x forward P/E and 5.7% FCF yield per MarketPal, with long-duration work in hand. But for this specific data-centre basket, the valuation discount is explained by weak thematic purity.

**Valuation stress:** If FY26 NPATA grows 7-10% from A$257.6m, implied NPATA is ~A$276-283m. At the MarketPal market cap of A$4.70bn, that is ~16.6-17.0x FY26 NPATA. If the market pays only 14x for a mature government-services contractor, equity value would be ~A$3.9bn, roughly 15-17% below current market cap before buyback offsets.

**Bull case:** Work in hand supports steady EPS growth; buyback improves per-share outcomes; telco/digital infrastructure work remains resilient.

**Bear case:** Contract renewal losses, labour inflation, or government budget tightening compress margins; the stock fails to get DC-theme multiple support.

**Evidence confidence:** **Medium** - company facts are adequate for the services business, but data-centre revenue is not quantified and MarketPal event data has a ticker-collision error.

**Cross-Company Read**

The key distinction is **asset owner vs contractor vs indirect services**.

IFT is the highest-quality exposure because CDC controls the scarce ingredients: land, power, hyperscale customers, long contracts and funding access. The catch is that IFT is a holdco and CDC’s growth requires very large capex before full billing.

SKS has the cleanest near-term contractor exposure. Its A$210m data-centre contract is huge relative to the company. That is also the risk: one or two large projects can dominate revenue, working capital and market sentiment.

SXE is the better balanced contractor. It has less purity than SKS but broader capabilities, a larger order book, net cash, facility headroom and a credible multi-discipline offer for data centres, batteries, rail and infrastructure.

VNT is a good business in a different debate. It may benefit from digital infrastructure and telco spend, but it is not a clean data-centre equity. In this group, it is the defensive infrastructure-services comparator, not the thematic winner.

**What Would Change The View**

1. **SXE:** confirmation of FY27 data-centre awards from the >A$1bn tender pipeline at acceptable margins would move it from best risk/reward to clear preferred contractor.
2. **SKS:** disclosure of contract margin, customer concentration, cash conversion and bank guarantee headroom after the A$210m project would either validate or break the valuation.
3. **IFT:** CDC funding terms, activation timetable and whether FY27 capex remains within A$3.8-4.2bn are the decisive NAV checks.
